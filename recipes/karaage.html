Karaage

Description

Hi everyone. Today, I'm showing you how to make delicious Karaage, a Japanese fried chicken.

It's a very simple recipe. And anyone can recreate my delicious Karaage at home. I'm explaining many things in the video. So please stay with me until the end. Every small effort will make your Karaage taste better. (description and recipe from Sushi By Kunihiro)

Ingredients

  • Chicken thigh 3pcs( 600g )
  • Salt ( a few pinches )
  • Black Pepper ( a few pinches )
  • Soy Sauce 2 Tbsp
  • Sake 2 Tbsp ( You can substitute it with white wine )
  • Sesame oil 1tsp
  • Garlic 2 cloves
  • Ginger 10g
  • Egg 1
  • Corn Starch 70g ( Potato and Tapioca starch are also fine)
  • All-purpose Flour 70g

Steps

  1. Wipe off the moisture on the chicken with paper towels
  2. Debone the chicken
  3. Cut the chicken into big bite-sized pieces
  4. Lay flat cut chicken in a bowl
  5. Season chicken with salt and black pepper with few pinches
  6. Grate 2 cloves of garlic over the chicken
  7. Grate 10 grams of ginger over the chicken
  8. Add 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  9. Add 2 tbsp of sake
  10. Lastly add 1 tsp of sesame oil
  11. Mix it well and let the chicken absorb the marinade
  12. Cover the chicken with plastic and let it rest outside the fridge for 20-30 minutes
  13. Get a separate bowl and put 70 grams of cornstarch and 70 grams of all-purpose flour in it, then mix it well
  14. After 20-30 minutes have passed break an egg over the chicken, then mix it well
  15. Coat the chicken with the starch and flour mixture
  16. After coating the chicken let it rest for a couple minutes and let the powder absorb the moisture on the chicken
  17. While waiting for the chicken, heat the cooking oil on a large pot. Fill the pot with oil up to 2 centimeters. Then turn the heat to medium and bring the oil temperature up to 170 degrees celsius
  18. Once the oil is hot turn the heat to medium low
  19. Check the oil's temperature using chopsticks. Wet the the tips of the chopsticks with water and wipe off the water with a paper towel. Then dip the tips of chopsticks in the oil and look at bubbles come out of the chopsticks. At 170 degrees celsius tiny bubbles will be visible and fewer at 160 degrees celsius and 180 ddegrees celsius, you will see bigger bubbles
  20. Put half the chicken into the oil. And deep-fry for 4 minutes
  21. During 4 minutes of deep-frying adjust to ideal temperature between 160 - 170 degrees celsius, set the heat on the stove between medium low to low heat
  22. During deep-frying, after 2 minutes turn the chicken over and deep-fry for 2 more minutes to deep-fry for a total of 4 minutes
  23. After 4 minutes take all the chicken pieces out of the oil and put them on a cooling rack
  24. Let the chicken rest for 4 minutes. While resting the karaage clean up the oil
  25. Then deep-fry the remaining chicken in the same way
  26. After finishing the 2nd batch turn the heat back on to medium heat to raise the oil temperature to 180 degrees celsius. Then check the temperature with chopsticks expect to see bigger bubbles at 180 degrees celsisus
  27. Put all the karaage back into the oil and then deep-fry for a minute until they are golden brown
  28. During deep-frying, after 30 seconds turn the chicken over and deep-fry for 30 more seconds to deep-fry for a total of 1 minute
  29. After 1 minute take the karaage out shake off the oil well, and put them on a cooling rack
  30. Drain the oil from the karaage for 1 minute
  31. Put karaage pieces on a plate. (Optional) then garnish with a lemon wedge and put a little bit of japanese mayonnaise on the side
  32. Serve and enjoy
